10 Beautiful Churches to Visit in Manila City This Holy Week (Continuation)
9. Malate Church
Address: 2000 M.H. Del Pilar St., Malate, Manila

One of the oldest church in the country, Malate Church, formally known as Our Lady of Remedies Parish, has undergone multiple restorations. Unlike other old Churches, its façade has not been freshly repainted and is very evident of the age. The inside, however, is a complete opposite; the altar itself is like a golden church.  The facing the church is the beautiful Rajah Sulayman Park, making Malate Church look like a lost temple in a jungle of trees when viewed from afar.

10. San Agustin Church
Address: 181 Gen. Luna St., Intramuros, Manila

San Agustin Church, also called Archdiocesan Shrine of Our Lady of Correa, is the only church in the country to have survived multiple earthquakes recorded, including the great earthquake in 1863.  Designated as a World Heritage Site and a National Historical Landmark, this Baroque styled church looks plain and old from the outside, but has a magnificent interior.  Its walls and ceilings are of “trompe l’oeil” painting, which is apparently an art technique which makes the subject appear three dimensional. It also houses the remains of prominent people in Philippine history including Juan Luna and Miguel Lopez de Legaspi.  Located in the historical Intramuros, San Agustin Church is a site to visit.
