10 Beautiful Churches to Visit in Manila City This Holy Week
Aside from our world-class beach resorts and our famous Filipino hospitality, Philippines is also known as a predominantly Catholic country, something we inherited after 300 years of being under the Spaniards.  Only third from Brazil and Mexico in terms of Catholic Population, 81.4% or over 81 million Filipinos are Catholics. Our culture has been heavily shaped by Catholicism to the point that some Catholic traditions, such as the Sinulog Festival, has become a tourist attraction in the country.  
Now, it’s that time of the year again.  As the Holy Week approaches, Catholics all over the country now prepare for its week-long rites & rituals, one of which is the Visita Iglesia (Spanish for “Church Visit”), a tradition where Catholics visit 14 churches (sometimes 7) as a gesture of Catholics joining Jesus’ in a vigil before his Passion and Death on the Cross.

This year, I have been privileged to be part of a charity project called Simbahan App, a mobile platform that populates and makes available locations, schedules and other information of Catholic Churches in the Country. Throughout my fieldwork, I was able to visit architecturally beautiful Churches which I never thought were just around the corner.  Allow me to share to you some of my selections which I will be presenting per Area so you might consider some during your Visita Iglesia this coming Holy Week. Let me start with one of the oldest City in the Philippines, the City of Manila.
1. Manila Cathedral
Address: Cabildo cor. Baeterio St., Intramuros, Manila

Formally called Manila Metropolitan Cathedral-Basilica and home of the Archbishop of Manila, Manila Cathedral has been a venue for Papal masses in the country, the most recent of which was celebrated by Pope Francis last January 26, 2015.  Manila Cathedral is a basilica dedicated to the Blessed Virgin Mary as our Lady of the Immaculate Conception. Aside from its Romanesque* design, I find the interior of the Cathedral a sight to watch because of its Italian inspired artworks and its beautifully designed stained glasses.

2. Binondo Church
Address: Plaza Lorenzo Ruiz, Binondo, Manila

Binondo Church is also known as Minor Basilica of Saint Lorenzo Ruiz (The 1st Philippines Saint) and Our Lady of the Most Holy Rosary Parish. Research dictates that got its architectural inspiration from the Spanish and European Baroque Style.  But what I really liked about this Church is that while the front has heavy shades of gray brick cement, its inside is actually nicely painted pink? & white.  The realistic paintings in the ceiling and the altar which looks like a façade of another church is really worth seeing.

For more church details, click here.
3. Quiapo Church
Address: 910 Plaza Miranda, Quiapo, Manila

Another Baroque Style inspired Church, Quiapo Church is one of the most famous Churches in the country mainly because it is home to the Black Nazarene, a popularly venerated statue of Jesus Christ carrying the cross, which many believe (myself included) has miraculous attributes. Officially known as Minor Basilica of the Black Nazarene, the outside of the church is painted creamy white with various statues of saints.  The inside is plain, simple and spacious focusing the Black Nazarene as the centerpiece.

For more church details, click here.
4. San Sebastian Church
Address: Plaza del Carmen, Quiapo, Manila

Situated in a peaceful & less hectic corner of Quiapo area resides the Basílica Menor de San Sebastiá, more commonly known as the San Sebastian Church.  With a Gothic architectural design, this church is a declared a National Cultural Landmark and National Cultural Treasure as this is the only all-steel church in Asia.  While it is made of steel, surprisingly, the interior of San Sebastian Church looks like it’s made of stone.  It’s a high-ceilinged church even made more stunning by its beautiful stained glass window and pointed, Gothic-inspired altar at its center. Above the main altar is an image of Our Lady of Mount Carmel.

For more church details, click here.
5. San Beda Church
Address: 638 Mendiola St., San Miguel, Manila

Formally known as Abbey of Our Lady of Montserrat, San Beda Church is the mother church of the Benedictine Monks in the Philippines.  At a single glance, the church’s facade is not easily noticeable as the Church is covered with trees and as it blends in with the building structures of the colleges and universities in Mendiola.  Similar to the San Sebastian Church, San Beda church is also designed using neo-gothic inspiration. But what really struck me the most was the inside.  The moment you enter, you will definitely be marveled by the magnificent paintings balanced by the refreshing colors of the interior.  Easily one of the most beautiful Churches in the country for me.

For more church details, click here.
6. Twin Churches of Sampaloc
Address: Jose Figueras St., Sampaloc, Manila

In the busy district of Sampaloc, resides two adjacent churches: (1) Archdiocesan Shrine of Our Lady of Loreto, and (2) Saint Anthony Shrine. As a predominantly Catholic country, one would expect Churches to spring in each barangay / municipality.  But, to literary be built adjacent to each other, that’s a rare one.  Coined as the Twin Churches of Sampaloc (Bustillos), both churches are administered by Franciscans.  Visiting this during your Visita Iglesia would be a time-saver as you’ll tick two counts in one area.

7. Santa Cruz Church
Address: Santa Cruz, Manila

Santa Cruz church is a Baroque-inspired Church located at Santa Cruz Manila. Originally built in the early 1600s, this church sustained major damages before its current reconstructed design.  The highlight for me is its façade: a wonderful play of brick design and white outline, bell towers, topped with a statue of Our Lady of the Pillar (the Church’s patroness). The plaza at the front also has beautiful fountain called the Carriedo Fountain.

For more church details, click here.
8. Ermita Church
Address: MH del Pilar St. cor. Flores St. Ermita, Manila, Philippines

The Ermita Church is home to the Marian image of Our Lady of Guidance (Nuestra Señora de Guia) which is considered as the oldest image in the Philippines. Formally known as Archdiocesan Shrine of Our Lady of Guidance, the church looks simple from the outside with its shades of white. However, the inside is magnificent to watch as the hanging chandeliers illuminates the nice paintings in the ceiling. Moreover, the center looks like the “heaven has opened and out comes the altar” through its wonderful background.
